Sai Rama Raju presents a Greek play by Sophocles.

King Oedipus by Sophocles

OEDIPUS.

What, all the prophecies of the gods, where are they? The man whom I feared, Polybus, has died...

MESSENGER.

Hear me, Oedipus, for I have brought news of joy: Polybus is dead!

OEDIPUS.

Dead, you say? How? By his own hand, or was it disease?

MESSENGER.

He died of old age and long years, not from any sickness.

JOCASTA.

[_Turning to the gods._] O hear this, and let us rejoice! The prophecies are false!

OEDIPUS.

[_Relieved._] At last, I am free from the prophecy that haunted me.

MESSENGER.

But, Oedipus, there is more to tell... you are not Polybus's true son.
